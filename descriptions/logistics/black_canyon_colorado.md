## Getting there
- Closest airport: Las Vegas
- Drive time: One hour outside the city
- Trailhead / entry point: Kayak entry points include either Hoover Dam or Willow Beach.

## Time & season
- Best months: Summer can be brutally hot but the river provides cool relief. Shoulder seasons such as March-May or Sept-Nov are the best overall.
- Typical start time: A bit more complicated than usual. While early starts are best for beating the heat, the famous emerald green water you are there for only appears at certain times of the day. See notes for more info.
- Permit / reservation: None

## What to pair it with
- Nearby stops: You'll probably be spending at least a few days in Las Vegas.
- Good multi-day combo: Red Rock Canyon, Valley of Fire, and Charleston Peak are all located with a few hours and each provide great views.

## Safety & prep
- Gear notes: Kayak/Paddleboard, Lifejacket, Sunscreen, Swimsuit.
- Water: Bring your own.
- Weather hazards: Heat is almost always the biggest factor. The canyon can also often act as a wind tunnel however making paddling against it a challenging expereince.

## Getting there
- Closest airport: Liberia
- Drive time: 90 minutes from either Liberia or La Fortuna depending on starting point.
- Trailhead / entry point: All trails within the park start from the Tenorio Volcano National Park visitor center.

## Time & season
- Best months: Dry season is the most likely to have the distinctive bright blue color as heavy rains can wash sediment causing it to turn dull brown.
- Typical start time: Early morning to beat the inevitable massive crowds. Try to arrive as it opens 8am.
- Permit / reservation: Costa Rica national parks entry required. Can be purchased online at SINAC and costs 13 - 16$.

## What to pair it with
- Nearby stops: Liberia or La Fortuna are typically where you will drive to this park from and likely return to afterwards.
- Good multi-day combo: Usually best when combined with other Costa Rica experiences like La Fortuna, the Pacific Coast, or Carribean Coast.

## Safety & prep
- Gear notes: Standard hiking gear, rain jacket, sunglasses, sunscreen, hat.
- Water: Bring your own.
- Weather hazards: During rainy season trail can become quite muddy and rocky, be prepared with shoes that have adequate grip.

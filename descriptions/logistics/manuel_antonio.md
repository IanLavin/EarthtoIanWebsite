## Getting there
- Closest airport: Juan Santamaría International Airport
- Drive time: 4-6 hours depending on traffic
- Trailhead / entry point: The parking situation at the park can be quite difficult. There are no official lots, only unofficial lots where uniformed men will attempt to get you to park there and pay them.

## Time & season
- Best months: Dry season to enjoy the beach and swimming. Typically Dec - April
- Typical start time: Early morning to beat crowds. Park also closes early at 3:00 PM.
- Permit / reservation: Costa Rica national park pass SINAC. Can be bought ahead of time online for 13 - 16$.

## What to pair it with
- Nearby stops: There are a number of other nice beaches along the coast including Jaco, Playa Herradura, and Playa Blanca.
- Good multi-day combo: Usually best combined with some inland rainforest exploration such as La Fortuna.

## Safety & prep
- Gear notes: Beach wear including sunscreen, hat, sunglasses, swimsuit, hiking sandels, and a towel.
- Water: Bring your own 
- Weather hazards: During the dry season temperatues are plesant and the sun abundant but hte UV is also quite intense this close to the equator. Apply sunscreen liberally.

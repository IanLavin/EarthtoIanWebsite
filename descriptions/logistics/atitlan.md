## Getting there
- Closest airport: Guatemala City
- Drive time: 3-4 hours depending on traffic.
- Trailhead / entry point: Nearly all trips will start at Panajachel and then require you to take a local ferry to one of the surrounding towns on the lake.

## Time & season
- Best months: Dry season produces the most plesant weather and best conditions on the lake. Dec - March.
- Typical start time: Early morning to enjoy a more calm lake with less of the common afternoon winds.
- Permit / reservation: None

## What to pair it with
- Nearby stops: There are many activites to do on the lake itself but you can also hike the nearby hills and volcanoes.
- Good multi-day combo: Usually paired as part of the well establised tourist route with Antigua.

## Safety & prep
- Gear notes: Standard lake gear including swimsuit, towel, sunscreen, hat, sunglasses.
- Water: Bring your own or buy bottled and most tap water is not safe to drink.
- Weather hazards: The lake has a well established pattern of afternoon winds known as Xocomil, which typically start after noon and cause the lake to become choppy and unplesant to travel across.

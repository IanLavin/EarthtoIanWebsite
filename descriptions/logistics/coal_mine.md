## Getting there
- Closest airport: Phoenix or Albuquerque
- Drive time: 4-5 hours depending on airport
- Trailhead / entry point: No offical trailhead or signs, just a small turnoff road from highway 264.

## Time & season
- Best months: Spring to Fall for clear weather and no snow.
- Typical start time: Flexible.
- Permit / reservation: Used to be required but recently removed. Check Navajo website before visiting.

## What to pair it with
- Nearby stops: Petrified forest and Grand Canyon are only a few hours drive away.
- Good multi-day combo: Best combined with nearby attractions in Arizona such as national parks, Flagstaff, Page, or Sedona.

## Safety & prep
- Gear notes: Standard Hiking Gear.
- Water: Bring more than you think you will need.
- Weather hazards: Road is undeveloped so rain can present issues with mud/getting stuck. Also exposed and open if a lightning storm rolls through.

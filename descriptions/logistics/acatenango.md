## Getting there
- Closest airport: Guatemala City
- Drive time: 2-3 hours depending on traffic.
- Trailhead / entry point: Nearly all hikes start from the same point in Aldea La Soledad.

## Time & season
- Best months: Dry season produces the most favorible climbing conditions and most likely to have a clear view of eruptions. Dec - April.
- Typical start time: Early morning to reach the base camp by afternoon and Fuego ridge by sunset. 10am or earlier.
- Permit / reservation: There is a fee to enter the park but this is typically included if you book with a tour company. For most tourists this also makes the most sense as it handles the logistics of arrival and overnight camping.

## What to pair it with
- Nearby stops: Most trips to the volcano start from Antigua which is a nice little colonial town with some interesting sights to see.
- Good multi-day combo: There is a well established tourist route that includes Antigua and Lake Atitlan. If you are ambitious you can also add on Tikal or Semuc Champey as well.

## Safety & prep
- Gear notes: Standard hiking gear, warm jacket for night, sunscreen, hat, sunglasses.
- Water: None available on volcano so bring all that you will need on your own.
- Weather hazards: Due to their prominence the volcano can attract clouds and storms. Be aware of the weather forcast and especially aware if you do the extra Fuego ridge hike and it is extremly exposed.

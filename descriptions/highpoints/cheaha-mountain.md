Cheaha Mountain is the highest point in Alabama and sits within Cheaha State Park amid the rolling forested ridges of the Talladega National Forest. The summit is crowned by a stone observation tower built by the Civilian Conservation Corps in the 1930s, which frames views across the densely wooded hills of east-central Alabama. At just over 2,400 feet it is a modest summit by Appalachian standards, but the tower and surrounding park make it a worthwhile stop.

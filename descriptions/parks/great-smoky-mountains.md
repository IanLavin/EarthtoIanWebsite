Great Smoky Mountains is the most visited national park in the country, straddling the Tennessee and North Carolina border along a ridge of ancient Appalachian peaks. The park is named for the blue-grey haze produced by the trees themselves, which gives the mountains a layered, atmospheric quality that shifts constantly with the weather. Waterfalls, black bears, diverse hiking trails, and the highest peak in the eastern US at Clingmans Dome make it one of the most complete parks on the East Coast, though its popularity means the crowds can be as relentless as the fog.

Gateway Arch National Park is the smallest and most urban park in the system, centered on Eero Saarinen's 630-foot stainless steel arch rising over the St. Louis riverfront. A tram ride to the top delivers sweeping views across the Mississippi River and the city skyline, while the museum beneath traces the story of westward expansion. It's less a wilderness escape than a monument you can walk inside, but its sheer geometric audacity earns it a place on the list.

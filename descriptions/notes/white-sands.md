- The characteristic white color of the dunes comes from gypsum and they are the largest collection of gypsum dunes in the world.
- The gypsum comes from the nearby mountains, where it eroded and flowed into a shallow lake, which eventually evaporated leaving the sediment behind.
- The park shares overlapping boundaries with the White Sands Missle Range, a US Army munitions testing area famous for being the site of the first atomic bomb test at the trinity site.
